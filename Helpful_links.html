Papers


There is no shortage of papers to read regarding different methods used for CyTOF. Some that I found useful are:

  1. Newell EW & Cheng Y. Mass cytometry: blessed with the curse of dimensionality. (2016) Nature Immunology 17, 890–895

  2. Saeys Y, Gassen SV & Lambrecht BN. Computational flow cytometry: helping to make sense of high-dimensional immunology data. (2016) Nature Reviews Immunology 16, 449–462

  3. R. Finck et al., Normalization of mass cytometry data with bead standards. (2013) Cytometry Part A. 83A, 483–494.

  4. R. Zunder et al., Palladium-based mass tag cell barcoding with a doublet-filtering scheme and single-cell deconvolution algorithm. (2015) Nat. Protocols. 10, 316–333

  5. Nowicka M. et al. CyTOF workflow: differential discovery in high-throughput high-dimensional cytometry datasets
